# Thread — Product Information

## What it is
Thread is a simple, single-user CRM for tracking contacts, logging interactions, and seeing a sales pipeline in one place. It is deliberately lightweight and fast — not an enterprise CRM.

## Who it's for
A solo user ("just me") managing their own contacts, interactions, and pipeline. There is no team, no sharing, no permissions, no multi-user concept anywhere. Built for one person, so every screen is tuned to how the owner personally works.

## The day-one hero (the ONE loop)
Logging and the pipeline board are a single loop, not two separate features:
1. Capture a contact in seconds.
2. Log calls, emails, meetings, and notes against that contact in seconds.
3. That activity feeds a visual pipeline board showing where every deal stands and what needs attention next.

This capture → log → see-the-pipeline loop is the day-one hero. Everything else is secondary. Optimize relentlessly for the speed and clarity of this loop.

## Core jobs
1. **Track contacts** — a clean, searchable list of people.
2. **Log interactions** — record calls, emails, meetings, and notes against each contact, fast.
3. **See the pipeline** — a visual board showing where deals/relationships stand across stages, with clear movement between stages.

## Strategic principle (most important)
The real value isn't the contact list — it's pipeline visibility. Deals rarely die from a bad pitch; they die from follow-ups that quietly slip. The product must make "what needs my attention next" obvious so nothing leaks. Surface stale deals, overdue follow-ups, and next actions prominently.

## Positioning & tone
- "Simple" is a hard requirement: lightweight, fast, low-friction.
- Favor clarity and low friction over feature breadth. When in doubt, cut.
- Calm, focused, confident. No clutter, no enterprise bloat, no jargon.
- Anti-references: Salesforce, heavy enterprise CRMs with deep config, permission matrices, and feature sprawl. Thread is the opposite.

## Scope guardrails (do NOT build)
- No team features, no sharing, no user/role management, no permissions.
- No email/calendar integrations for v1 — logging is manual and fast.
- No reporting suites or dashboards beyond what serves "what needs attention next."
Keep the surface small and the loop tight.
